The Perpetual City

By Vera @ GroundedWren.com

There’s a place where rusted metal sheets
Line the hollow buildings row by row
You can walk the crumbled asphalt streets
They’ll guide you where the tired people go
Up a block the lanes are lined with gold
Silver creeks and sleek glass mountains meet
Wonders rise while founding bricks grow old
All the kids have new shoes on their feet
Strolling down the borderline I see
An older man with creaky bike draw near
I smile at him and he nods back at me
We say hello too soft for ears to hear
