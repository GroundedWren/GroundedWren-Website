Nakedness

By Vera @ GroundedWren.com

It’s funny to wear clothes
Shirts and long-dried hide
Toupees and pristine bows
We’re all naked inside
We think we make our thoughts
That we decide our fate
All we know and all we’re taught
Just three pounds in weight
There’s a train upon its track
Going far and wide
A steering wheel it does lack
The path it must abide
While flesh is new and young
The human mind is old
It bites the nails and holds the tongue
It’s calm and dark and cold
You cannot see a rose
The mind itself is blind
Your senses speak in prose
And lies on lies you’ll find
In dark and troubled times
When man does choose to sin
Those evil deeds and heinous crimes
Live deep beneath our skin
We cover up and move along
We’re different now these days
But in our brains an ancient song
Plays with us always
